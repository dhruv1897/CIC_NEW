# PULSE — From a News Page to a Real Business

*A practical roadmap. Honest about money, time, and effort.*

## 1. The whole idea, in plain terms

You have a daily news product. It takes the most important stories from across the web — world, business, tech, markets, crypto, science, health, sports, culture — and packages them into one fast, skimmable place. The value you're selling isn't the news itself (anyone can find news). The value is **curation and time saved**: busy people pay attention to a source that reliably tells them "here's what matters today" in two minutes.

That's the real business: you become a **trusted daily habit**. Habits are what make money in media — they create a returning audience, and a returning audience is what advertisers, sponsors, and subscribers pay for.

The HTML page I built is your **product demo and homepage**. It proves the concept. The business is built around turning that page into a daily ritual people come back to across a website, an email newsletter, and social channels.

## 2. How this actually earns money

You have four revenue streams, and they turn on at different audience sizes. Don't expect all four on day one — they stack as you grow.

**Sponsorships — works earliest, even small.** A brand pays to put their message in your daily briefing. At 1,000–5,000 engaged subscribers you can charge $50–$300 per send. This is the fastest first dollar because you can sell it directly without any ad network approving you.

**Display ads — scales with traffic.** Once the website gets visitors, ad networks pay you per thousand views. Google AdSense starts immediately but pays modestly. At ~50,000 monthly visits you qualify for premium networks (Mediavine, Raptive) that pay 5–10x more. This is passive once set up but needs real traffic to matter.

**Paid subscriptions — highest value per person.** Free briefing for everyone; a paid tier ($5–$10/month) for extras: the full archive, an ad-free version, a deeper morning report, or niche briefings (e.g. "Crypto PULSE"). Even 200 paying members at $6/month is ~$1,200/month recurring. This is the most durable, most valuable revenue once you have a loyal core.

**Affiliate / commerce — steady side income.** When stories mention products, tools, books, or services, you link with affiliate codes (Amazon, Impact, ShareASale) and earn a commission on sales. Low effort, compounds with traffic.

**Rough revenue picture as you grow:**

| Stage | Audience | Likely monthly revenue | Main drivers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Launch (0–3 mo) | 0–1,000 | $0–$100 | First sponsor, AdSense |
| Traction (3–9 mo) | 1,000–10,000 | $200–$2,000 | Sponsors + subs + ads |
| Growth (9–18 mo) | 10,000–50,000 | $2,000–$10,000 | Premium ads + subs scale |
| Scale (18 mo+) | 50,000+ | $10,000+ | All four streams compound |

These are realistic ranges, not guarantees. Media is a slow build — the people who win are the ones who publish **every single day** without missing.

## 3. What turns this from a page into a business

Three things separate a hobby page from a company:

**A pipeline, not manual work.** You can't hand-paste news daily forever. You connect a news API (or RSS feeds) so the page refreshes automatically each morning. That's the single most important upgrade — it makes the product run itself.

**A niche or a voice.** "All the news" competes with Google, Apple News, and every major outlet — that's a hard fight. The winners pick an angle: a region (e.g. "the daily briefing for India's startup scene"), a topic (AI, crypto, business), or a personality (your take, your voice). A sharp niche grows far faster than a generic feed. **This is your most important strategic decision.**

**An owned audience.** Website traffic can vanish if Google changes its rules. An email list is yours forever — no algorithm can take it away. That's why the newsletter is the heart of the business, even though the website is the front door.

## 4. Your first 90 days (concrete steps)

**Weeks 1–2 — Make it real.**
Pick a name and a niche. Buy a domain (~$10/yr at Cloudflare or Namecheap). Host the page free on Vercel, Netlify, or Cloudflare Pages — it'll be live worldwide in minutes. Set up a free email tool (Beehiiv is the best for monetizing later) and connect the subscribe box.

**Weeks 3–4 — Automate the news.**
Wire the page to a news API (NewsAPI, GNews, or free RSS feeds) so it updates daily. Add a scheduled job (Vercel Cron or GitHub Actions, both free) to refresh every morning. Now the product runs without you.

**Weeks 5–8 — Publish daily and grow the list.**
Send the briefing by email every morning, no exceptions — consistency is everything. Post 2–3 headlines a day to one social channel (pick X, Instagram, or LinkedIn — whichever fits your niche) to pull people to the signup. Goal: first 500–1,000 subscribers.

**Weeks 9–12 — Turn on money.**
Apply for Google AdSense on the site. Add affiliate links where natural. Once you pass ~1,000 engaged readers, sell your first sponsor slot directly (reach out to relevant small brands/tools in your niche). Launch a simple $5/month premium tier.

## 5. How to grow after that

Grow the audience by being **findable and shareable**. SEO matters: each story summary is a page Google can index, so good headlines and tags bring free search traffic over time. Make every briefing easy to forward and post. Add a "refer a friend" reward in the newsletter (Beehiiv has this built in) — referrals are the cheapest growth there is.

Deepen the product as you scale: add more niche briefings (each one a new audience and a new ad/sub surface), a weekly deep-dive, maybe a short daily audio or video version for social. Each format multiplies reach from the same research you're already doing.

Reinvest early revenue into **distribution** — a small ad budget to grow the email list, or paying a writer so you can publish more — rather than taking it out. Media compounds: a list that grows 10% a month is a very different business in a year.

## 6. Costs and the honest risks

**Startup cost is tiny** — realistically $0–$30/month to begin (domain + free tiers of everything). It scales only as you grow (a news API and email tool move to paid tiers as your volume rises, maybe $50–$300/month later — by then you should have revenue covering it).

**The real cost is time and consistency.** This rewards showing up daily for months before the money is meaningful. Most people quit at month two. The ones who don't, win.

**Legal reality — important.** Republishing others' full articles for profit is copyright infringement. The safe, sustainable model is what your page already does: write your **own short summary** and **link to the source**. Don't copy article text. As you grow, you can license real feeds (AP, Reuters) or keep building on original summaries and your own commentary.

**Don't over-promise to readers or sponsors.** Accuracy is your whole brand. One viral false story can undo months of trust. Verify before you publish.
